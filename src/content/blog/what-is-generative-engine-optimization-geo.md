Generative Engine Optimization, often shortened to GEO, is the practice of improving how content performs inside AI systems that generate answers, summaries, and recommendations.

## GEO vs SEO vs AEO

These terms overlap, but they are not identical.

- SEO focuses on organic visibility in search engines.
- AEO focuses on answer systems that surface direct responses.
- GEO focuses on the generative layer where models retrieve, combine, and present information.

The practical overlap is large, but GEO is a useful label because it highlights the synthesis step.

## What GEO tries to influence

A GEO program tries to improve:

- retrieval probability
- source selection
- citation likelihood
- brand recall inside answers

That is why GEO work usually touches technical SEO, content structure, entity clarity, and digital PR at the same time.

## GEO principles that are working

### Publish pages with a single dominant job

One page should solve one primary problem well.

### Make claims easy to verify

Support statements with data, examples, or explicit reasoning.

### Strengthen your entity footprint

Keep brand, author, and topical associations clear across the site.

### Build clusters, not isolated pages

Generative systems interpret sites as topic networks, not just standalone URLs.

GEO is a useful term because it reminds teams to optimize for generated answers, not only ranked pages.
